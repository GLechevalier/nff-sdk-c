% ===== TITLE ===============================================================
{\large\textbf{nff on battery --- ESP32-WROOM-32E, BLE-only sensor node}}\hfill
{\footnotesize Rev.\ A --- 2026-07-14}\\[0.3mm]
{\scriptsize\itshape Target: \SI{6000}{\milli\ampere\hour}, 12 months, wake every \SI{5}{\minute},
BLE-advertise to one gateway per site. No WiFi/LTE on the device.}\\[-2mm]
\rule{\textwidth}{0.4pt}

\vspace{1mm}

% ===== VERDICT =============================================================
\fcolorbox{warn}{white}{\parbox{\dimexpr\textwidth-2\fboxsep-2\fboxrule}{\scriptsize
\textbf{VERDICT --- the spec as written fails, and no measurement can rescue it.} Grant every
advantage: no battery derate (\SI{6000}{\milli\ampere\hour} $\to$ \SI{685}{\micro\ampere} mean
budget), a \emph{free} sleep ($I_\text{sleep}=0$), radio \emph{off}. The duty cycle is fixed by the
requirement at $\SI{30}{\second}/\SI{300}{\second}=\SI{10}{\percent}$, so
$I_\text{awake,max}=\SI{685}{\micro\ampere}/0.10=\bad{\SI{6.85}{\milli\ampere}}$. \textbf{No ESP32
executes code at \SI{6.85}{\milli\ampere}} --- Espressif's floor (modem sleep, \SI{80}{\mega\hertz},
radio off) is \SIrange{20}{31}{\milli\ampere}. \bad{Over budget by $\geq3\times$} before the sensor
is read. \emph{Three numbers, none of them ours: your \si{\milli\ampere\hour}, your duty cycle,
Espressif's datasheet.}\\[0.4mm]
\textbf{The fix is time, not milliamps.} ``Awake \SI{30}{\second}'' $\neq$ ``CPU runs
\SI{30}{\second}'' --- a \good{$12\times$} difference in battery life, and the spec is silent on
which one gets built. \textbf{Ceiling: the CPU may run for $\mathbf{\leq\SI{3.9}{\second}}$ per
\SI{5}{\minute} cycle}
$\;[\,t_\text{on,max}=T(I_\text{budget}-I_\text{sleep})/(I_\text{awake}-I_\text{sleep})\,]$.
}}

D2 and D3 land within \SI{5}{\percent} of each other --- \textbf{do not choose between them on
battery life.} Take D3 only if one gateway must round-robin many nodes, or if the \SI{30}{\second}
window is contractual. \textbf{Once the CPU sleeps, \emph{boot $+$ init} --- not the radio --- is the
largest line item.} \bad{The two paths have different BOMs: decide before layout.}
}

\vspace{0.6mm}
\textbf{OTA is not the problem} --- \meas\,\SI{0.41}{\milli\ampere\hour}, \SI{0.007}{\percent} of the
battery. \bad{WiFi is.} As the SDK ships (it never calls \texttt{esp\_wifi\_set\_ps()}) an
associated ESP32 idles at \meas\,\SI{49.7}{\milli\ampere}: \bad{4.3 days} on this cell. The
default \SI{30}{\second} heartbeat alone costs \SI{1577}{\milli\ampere\hour}/yr,
\SI{26}{\percent} of the battery. \textbf{BLE-only was not a preference --- it is the only
transport that closes.}
}

% ===== SLEEP FLOOR (callout) ==============================================
\fcolorbox{warn}{white}{\parbox{\dimexpr\columnwidth-2\fboxsep-2\fboxrule}{\scriptsize
\textbf{2 --- The sleep floor is a \emph{board} property, not a chip property}\,\mock\\[0.3mm]
\textbf{Custom \good{\SI{20}{\micro\ampere}}}: module \SI{10}{\micro\ampere} (deep sleep, RTC timer)
$+$ low-$I_q$ LDO \SI{1.6}{\micro\ampere} $+$ sensor standby \& leakage \SI{8}{\micro\ampere}. That
is \SI{3.4}{\percent} of budget.\\[0.3mm]
\textbf{Devkit \bad{\SI{6.5}{\milli\ampere}}}: AMS1117 \bad{\SI{5000}{\micro\ampere}} $+$ power LED
\bad{\SI{1400}{\micro\ampere}} $+$ CP2102 \SI{100}{\micro\ampere} $+$ the above ---
\bad{$11\times$ the entire per-cycle budget, asleep.}\\[0.3mm]
\bad{A devkit fails the requirement while doing nothing at all.} No firmware recovers this; any
battery-life figure measured on one is meaningless.}}

% ===== TABLE 3: nff SDK settings ==========================================
{\scriptsize
\textbf{3 --- Optimal nff SDK settings under a power constraint}\\[0.5mm]
\bad{First: the SDK cannot run on this device today.} Verified in source --- \textbf{no BLE}
(\texttt{nff\_port.h} exposes only \texttt{nff\_port\_mqtt\_*} and \texttt{\_https\_get\_stream};
zero NimBLE/bluedroid hits in any firmware) and \textbf{no sleep} (no \texttt{esp\_deep\_sleep} /
\texttt{esp\_pm\_*} anywhere).\\[0.8mm]
\begin{tabular}{@{}p{26mm}p{11mm}p{40mm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Symbol} & \textbf{Set} & \textbf{Why} \\
\midrule
\texttt{heartbeat\_} \texttt{interval\_s} & $30\to\mathbf{300}$ & The only shipped power knob (Kconfig max). One heartbeat per wake. \\
\texttt{NFF\_MQTT\_} \texttt{BUFFER\_SIZE} & $\mathbf{1024}$ & \bad{A landmine, not a knob.} The OTA cmd is 420\,B; PubSubClient's 256\,B default drops it \emph{silently}. \\
\texttt{NFF\_TIMESTAMP\_} \texttt{WINDOW\_S} & $300\to\mathbf{3600}$ & No SNTP when duty-cycled; the RTC's internal RC drifts $\pm\SI{5}{\percent}$. At 300 it rejects valid commands. \\
\texttt{NFF\_LOG\_LINES} $\times$\texttt{\_LINE\_LEN} & $\mathbf{8\times96}$ & Lives in \texttt{RTC\_NOINIT} --- retained through deep sleep, so it costs standby current. \\
\texttt{NFF\_OTA\_MAX\_} \texttt{DL\_RETRIES} & $4\to\mathbf{2}$ & Each retry is a fresh TLS handshake. Die fast; retry next cycle. \\
\texttt{NFF\_OTA\_MIN\_} \texttt{HEALTHY\_MS} & \textbf{5000} & \textbf{Unchanged.} The \SI{5}{\second} soak \emph{exceeds} the \SI{3.9}{\second} ceiling --- budget a long-wake exception. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\vspace{0.6mm}
\bad{Two hardcoded behaviours must become configurable.} \textbf{(a)} The reconnect ladder
(\texttt{nff\_core.c:31}) saturates at \SI{60}{\second} and retries \emph{forever}:
\SI{57}{\micro\ampere\hour} an attempt $=$ \bad{\SI{500}{\milli\ampere\hour}/month} against a dead
gateway, with no telemetry to show for it --- \textbf{the SDK's worst battery failure mode.}
\textbf{(b)} SNTP (\texttt{nff\_port\_esp32\_arduino.c:60}) is unconditional and blocks
\texttt{nff\_connect()} up to \SI{20}{\second} on every cold boot: \bad{$5\times$ the entire
CPU-active budget}, every cycle.

\vspace{0.6mm}
\textbf{To build:} \texttt{nff\_port\_ble\_adv\_*}, \texttt{nff\_port\_sleep\_deep/light()},
\texttt{nff\_run\_once()}, RTC\_NOINIT state carry, a packed 27-B codec, a gateway MQTT bridge.
\textbf{The PAL is the clean seam --- an addition, not a rewrite.}
}

\vspace{0.6mm}
\textbf{The 31-byte advert.} BLE 4.2 legacy; \bad{no extended advertising on this part --- 31\,B,
not 255}. MSD framing 4\,B $\Rightarrow$ \textbf{27\,B payload}: \texttt{ver}\,1 $+$ \texttt{seq}\,2
$+$ \texttt{flags}\,1 $+$ \texttt{fw\_id}\,2 $+$ \texttt{batt}\,1 $+$ \textbf{sensor 16} $+$ CMAC
tag 4 --- exactly full. \textbf{Identity is free:} the 6-B static address is already in the header.
The nff heartbeat (250-B JSON) \bad{does not fit, by $8\times$} --- the gateway must
\emph{reconstruct} it. \bad{Bluedroid's \texttt{config\_adv\_data()} silently inserts a 3-B Flags
AD} --- use \texttt{\_raw()}, or the sensor field shrinks from 16\,B to 13\,B.
}

% ===== FOOTER: what's real =================================================
{\scriptsize
\mock\ \bad{MOCK.} \textbf{Every current and duration above is invented} --- plausible, internally
consistent, \emph{not measured}. \textbf{Real:} everything about the SDK (read from source), the
BLE 4.2 / ESP32 capability limits, and the arithmetic. \textbf{The verdict does not depend on the
mocks} (see box); what the mocks \emph{do} control is the D2/D3 margin --- 18 months, or 11, or 30.
\emph{Do not promise a customer 12 months on this table yet.}
\;\textbf{One measurement converts this sheet:} it all hinges on $I_\text{awake}$, which is tens of
\si{\milli\ampere} --- \emph{inside} the shunt rig's range (only the \SI{20}{\micro\ampere} sleep
floor is not; that needs a PPK2/INA228, and the rig's own datasheet says so). Flash a bare BLE
advertiser --- no nff, no WiFi, no sensor --- mount per \texttt{datasheet.tex} (\bad{DUT USB
\emph{not} connected}), run \texttt{nff power measure -{}-for 30}. \bad{Run \texttt{WIRECHECK} and
Phase-0 calibration first} --- Phase 0 has never been done, so the rig has no $R_\text{eff}$ and
will report a confident, wrong number (wired to \emph{nothing}, it once read
\SI{590}{\milli\ampere}).
\;\textbf{If the BOM is not frozen, change the module:} an ESP32-C3/C6 is the same price and
footprint class, with BLE 5.0 (extended adv $\Rightarrow$ 255-B payload, so the nff heartbeat fits
\emph{as-is}) and $\approx$half the active current. \emph{The design closes on the WROOM-32E
anyway} --- at $1.5\times$ margin instead of $\approx3\times$. A recommendation, not a blocker.
